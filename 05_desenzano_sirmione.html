Sirmione. Hotel check-in.

Hotel Sirmione Terme: Piazza Castello 19. Right in the old town, spa and outdoor pool on site. Walk through the Scaliger Castle gate to get to the hotel — yes, through a medieval castle.

5:00
Aquaria Thermal Spa

The main spa event — on the lake.

Aquaria Thermal Spa (5-min walk) is built right over the lake, with outdoor thermal pools where you're swimming over the water with views across to the mountains. Indoor pools too. Relax circuit. Entry is separate from the hotel (~€20/person). Book ahead in high season — it fills up.

Sunset from the outdoor pool at Aquaria is one of the best moments in northern Italy.

8:00
Dinner

Old town dinner.

Sirmione's centro storico is tiny but has good restaurants. Ristorante La Fiasca for traditional Garda fish (lavarello, persico, trota). Al Pescatore for lake fish right on the water. Book ahead — the peninsula has limited restaurant space in season.

10:00
Night walk

Scaliger Castle lit at night.

The castle and the tip of the peninsula are extraordinary at night with no crowds. Walk out to the very end of Sirmione — the lake on both sides, lights of Desenzano across the water. Quiet and beautiful after the day-tripper buses have left.

Grotte di Catullo — Roman ruins.

The largest Roman villa ruins in northern Italy, at the very tip of the Sirmione peninsula. Occupied from the 1st century BC to the 3rd century AD. Walk through olive groves with lake on both sides to get there. ~€6 entry. Morning before the crowds — the best time to go.

Things worth knowing — Sirmione specifics.

  • Sirmione traffic restriction. The peninsula has a ZTL (restricted traffic zone) for most of it. Only hotel guests and residents can drive in. If you ever think about renting a car for this, don't — the bus and walk-in system is how it works.
  • Aquaria needs advance booking. On summer weekends Aquaria sells out. Go to termediSirmione.it before the trip and book your Saturday afternoon slot online. Saves disappointment on arrival.
  • Crowds peak July–August. The peninsula is narrow and the day-trippers are significant in peak season. Stay overnight and you have the town to yourself from ~7 PM onwards. One of the best reasons to actually sleep there rather than day-trip.
  • Scaliger Castle. ~€6 to enter and climb. The towers give you views over the entire peninsula and both lake directions. Optional but worth it for 45 minutes.
  • Lake swimming at Sirmione. There are small beaches on the east side of the peninsula. Lake Garda water is swimmable from late June onwards (~23°C). The thermal springs near the tip of the peninsula mean the water is slightly warmer than the rest of the lake in places.
  • Ferry option Sunday. Ferries connect Sirmione/Desenzano to other Garda towns (Garda, Malcesine, Limone). If you want a spontaneous Sunday morning extension, catch the 10 AM ferry to Lazise or Garda town and back — just confirm return times fit your train home.
  • Tourist tax paid in cash at checkout — a few euros per person per night.
