Terme Euganee–Abano-Montegrotto.

Out of the station, walk 5 minutes to Piazza Roma 19 — Relilax is right there. Mostly flat, easy with the small bag.

4:00
Check-in

Hotel check-in.

Standard check-in is 2:30 PM, so you're well within window. Drop bags. Meet the Guest Relations Manager at the spa reception (Giardino Fiorito) — they'll walk you through the wellness areas.

Bathrobe and slippers are in the room. Save the bathrobe for the breakfast room only — not a wear-around-the-hotel garment.

4:30
Spa

Three full hours in the wellness area.

Hit the rotation — soak in Le Palme (outdoor, palm-tree pool), then the indoor Ardesia thermal pool, then Le Bolle minipool whirlpool. When you've had enough water, cycle through the saunas: Finnish, bio, steam bath, and the aromatherapy showers. Relaxation room between sessions.

Hydrate. Thermal pools dehydrate faster than you think.

7:30
Dinner

Dinner at Ginco — included in your half-board.

The hotel's restaurant. Italian local cuisine. You'll want a clothes change first (no bathrobe in the dining room). Wine pairings extra; ask about Colli Euganei DOC — they're the local wines from these very hills.

9:30
Night swim

Back to the pool, under the stars.

Le Palme is open until 11:30 PM. Outdoor pool, heated, palm trees, much quieter at night. This is the part of the trip Levico can't give you.

11:00
Sleep

Bed.

You'll sleep harder than you have in weeks. That's the thermal water doing its job.

Things worth knowing — for May 23–24.

  • Pentecost Sunday is May 24. Not a public holiday in Italy, but the Veneto can be slightly busier with Italian and Austrian/German weekenders. Book Relilax early; they're popular with the German-speaking spa crowd.
  • Adults only — 16+. Quieter spa, more couples, more romantic vibe. No kids running around the pool, ever.
  • Bathrobe etiquette. The hotel allows bathrobes only in the breakfast room and spa areas, never in common areas or restaurant. Pack a real outfit for dinner.
  • Treatment cancellations require 24 hours notice. If you book the optional €152 package, lock in your treatment slot before arrival via email — popular times sell out.
  • Dress code at Ginco. Smart casual — collared shirt for him, dress or smart pants for her. Not formal, but no shorts or pool wear.
  • Tourist tax at Italian hotels is a few euros per person per night, paid in cash at checkout, separate from your Relilax total.
  • Apps to install: Trenitalia for tickets (validate by buying in-app), Google Maps with offline data downloaded for Montegrotto.
  • If you want a treatment but didn't pre-book the package — walk-in availability for single massages is usually fine; ask the Guest Relations Manager at check-in. Single 50-min massage is ~€80–100 and you can charge it to the room.
  • Free bikes are first-come, first-served at reception. Ask Saturday afternoon if you want to ride Sunday morning, so they're held for you.
  • Hydrate before, during, after. Thermal water is mineral-heavy and warm — easy to overdo. The lounge bar has free water; refill at the saunas too.
