One bag. Swimsuit priority. Bring a light layer — Merano evenings are cooler than the daytime temps suggest.

Earlier train on this one. Leave home at 10:15, walk to Vicenza station, train at 11:00.

Vicenza → Verona → Bolzano → Merano.

2.5h of increasingly beautiful scenery. The Brenner valley section is genuinely stunning. Snacks on the train — you'll want them.

1:40
Arrive

Merano station. Walk to hotel.

~15 min walk through the town center along the Passirio River. You'll immediately notice the Mediterranean-Alpine mix. Palm trees on the promenade, snowy peaks above.

2:00
Lunch

Late lunch in Merano.

You didn't eat at home today — have a proper sit-down. Café Gilm near the hotel is ideal. Or the hotel restaurant at Adria if you want to check in first. South Tyrolean specialties: Speckknödel (bacon dumplings), Schlutzkrapfen (spinach ravioli), Graukäse (grey cheese).

3:30
Check-in

Hotel Adria. Drop bags. Spa prep.

Via Gilm 2. 4-star, 9.0/10 on Booking. Indoor heated pool, sauna, Turkish steam bath, hot tub. One of the highest-rated hotels in all of Merano.

4:00
Terme Merano

The main event — 25 pools across 5,000 m².

Walk 15 min from hotel OR take the hotel's complimentary bike. Terme Merano has 12 indoor and 13 outdoor pools, a rooftop pool with Dolomite views, waterslides, Finnish sauna complex, hot thermal baths, steam rooms, and a kids section (ignore). Get a day pass at the entrance.

The rooftop pool at sunset is the defining Merano moment. Time it right.

7:30
Dinner

South Tyrolean dinner in the old town.

Pizzeria Castello for great pizza and local wine (casual, no reservations needed). Or Ristorante Sissi for proper Austrian-Italian fine dining. Order the local Lagrein wine — dark, earthy, completely unlike anything from the Veneto.

9:30
Evening walk

Tappeiner Promenade at night.

5km clifftop path above the town. Lit at night. Subtropical plants, views over the valley. One of the most surreal evening walks in Italy — you're on a cliff, it's warm, there are palm trees, and you can see glaciers.

Hotel Adria breakfast.

South Tyrolean breakfast culture is serious: fresh Vinschgauer bread, local Speck, regional cheeses, Apfelstrudel. Take your time.

Trauttmansdorff Gardens — the morning's highlight.

12-hectare botanical gardens built into the cliffside above Merano. Castle Trauttmansdorff at the top. Panoramic views of the valley and peaks from every terrace. The most beautiful 2 hours in Merano.

Alternative if you'd rather pool again: Hotel Adria's indoor pool is quieter Sunday morning and stays available until checkout.

Things worth knowing — Merano specifics.

  • Terme Merano entry price. Day passes vary by season (~€22–32/person). Check the current rate at termemerano.it before going. The rooftop pool is the move — time it for late afternoon when the peaks catch the light.
  • South Tyrol = bilingual. Every sign, menu, and train announcement is in Italian and German. Hotel staff and restaurant workers usually speak English, but a few words of Italian or German helps. Merano is formally Italian but culturally Austrian — don't be surprised if "Guten Tag" gets a warmer response than "Buongiorno."
  • Trauttmansdorff Gardens. Open April–November. Closed in winter. Check trauttmansdorff.org for current hours and pricing before booking the trip around them.
  • Tappeiner Way in spring. Wisteria blooms in April-May along the promenade. If you go in spring, this is genuinely one of the most beautiful 5km in Italy.
  • Bolzano layover. If you have 30+ min at Bolzano on the way, Bolzano's Piazza Walther is 5 min from the station and worth a quick look — stunning cathedral, mountain backdrop.
  • South Tyrol wine. Order local at dinner. Lagrein (dark red) and Gewürztraminer (aromatic white) are the signature grapes. You won't find them like this anywhere outside the region.
  • Hotel bikes. Hotel Adria provides free rental bikes. Use them for the ~15 min ride to Terme Merano instead of walking in the afternoon heat.
