Spa & Thermal

✓ Ready
No. 01
Thermal spa
~45 min

Montegrotto
Terme di Relilax

5-star adults-only boutique hotel with thermal pools, saunas, and the best breakfast buffet in the Veneto
Three thermal pools open until 11:30 PM. Outdoor Le Palme pool (palm trees, silver-ion water), indoor Ardesia, whirlpool Bolle. Half-board includes dinner at Ginco restaurant. Night swim under stars.
Train: Vicenza → Padova → Terme Euganee ~45 min

Thermal spa
~45 min

Abano Terme
Hotel Savoia Thermae

Italy's most famous spa town — dozens of hotels with thermal pools, all fed by volcanic springs
Smaller and quieter than Relilax but rated 9.4/10. Multiple pools, proper spa circuit, and the entire town is spa-themed so the vibe extends outside the hotel. Walk everywhere.
Train: Vicenza → Padova → Terme Euganee ~45 min

Wellness
Alpine lake
~2.5h

Levico Terme
Bellavista Relax Hotel

4-star alpine spa hotel on the Valsugana line — the lake is a 10-min walk from your room
Pool, spa, sauna complex in a Habsburg-era building. The lake loop walk is flat and beautiful. WWI fort up the hill for a half-day detour. Quieter and more alpine than Abano.
Train: Vicenza → Bassano → Levico ~2.5h

Thermal spa
Alpine town
~2.5h

Merano
Terme Merano

Alpine spa town where palm trees somehow grow under Dolomite peaks — Trauttmansdorff gardens and a legendary thermal complex
Terme Merano complex has 25 indoor/outdoor pools. The town itself has a Riviera Mediterranean feel with mountain views. The botanical gardens at Castle Trauttmansdorff are world-class.
Train: Vicenza → Verona → Bolzano → Merano ~2.5h
$$$

Lake Garda
~1h

Sirmione
Villa Cortine Palace

Lake Garda peninsula with a Scaliger castle at one end and Roman ruins at the other — thermal spa in the water
Sirmione has its own thermal spa right on the lake (Aquaria Thermal Spa). The peninsula is entirely walkable. Train to Desenzano, then 10-min bus to Sirmione. Castle views from the pool.
Train: Vicenza → Desenzano → bus ~1h 15min

Riva del Garda
Hotel Centrale

The dramatic north end of Lake Garda, hemmed in by thousand-metre cliffs — completely different feel from the south
Lakeside promenade, the Bastione viewpoint hike (45 min, stunning), Cascata del Varone waterfall 2km away, windsurfing. Compact and walkable from the hotel. Best in May–September.
Train: Vicenza → Verona → Rovereto → bus ~2h

Belluno
Gateway to Dolomiti

Charming alpine city with the Dolomiti Bellunesi National Park starting at the edge of town
Historic center with mountain views from every street. Local bus to Feltre, or walk the riverside Piave trail. Quieter and more local than Cortina. Stay at Hotel Delle Alpi in the centro storico.
Train: Vicenza → Conegliano → Belluno ~2h

Lago di Caldonazzo
Caldonazzo Village

Bigger sister to Levico — warmer swimming lake in summer, flatter terrain, more beach infrastructure
Valsugana line drops you steps from the lake. Clear mountain water, kayak rentals, cycle path around the perimeter. Quieter and more Italian than Garda. Combine with a Trento afternoon.
Train: Vicenza → Bassano → Caldonazzo ~2h

Trento
MUSE & Buonconsiglio

Italy's most alpine city — frescoed piazzas, Renzo Piano-designed science museum, and mountains visible from every street
Walk from the station into a city that feels half-Italian, half-Austrian. The MUSE science museum is genuinely world-class. Buonconsiglio Castle has stunning frescoes. Cable car up to Sardagna for free views.
Train: Vicenza → Verona → Trento ~1.5h

Peschiera del Garda
Lake & Fortress

The easiest Lake Garda access — UNESCO Venetian fortress, walkable from the station, ferries to Sirmione and the whole lake
Train drops you inside the historic walls. Walk along the lake, catch a ferry to Lazise or Sirmione, eat fish grilled lakeside. Relaxed vibe, not touristy. Hotel right on the lake ramparts.
Train: Vicenza → Peschiera del Garda ~1h 15min

Bologna
La Grassa

Italy's food capital — ragu, tortellini in brodo, mortadella, and 60 kilometres of medieval porticoes
Climb the Asinelli Tower. Walk 60km of UNESCO porticoes (or just admire them). Eat tagliatelle al ragù at a proper trattoria. Piazza Maggiore at dusk. Best food city in Italy, full stop.
Train: Vicenza → Verona → Bologna AV ~1.5h

Trieste
The Adriatic Edge

Italy's most un-Italian city — Habsburg coffee houses, Miramare Castle on the Adriatic, and a seafront piazza the size of a football field
Piazza Unità d'Italia opens directly to the sea. Miramare Castle on a rocky promontory 15 min by bus. Historic Viennese-style caffès. Slovenia is a 30-min bus ride. Completely different from anything else on this list.
Train: Vicenza → Venice → Trieste ~2h 30min

Ferrara
The Walled City

A perfectly preserved Renaissance city inside complete medieval walls — everyone rides bikes and nobody crowds it
Moated Estense Castle right in the center. Rent bikes and ride the complete city wall circuit (9km, flat). Palazzo dei Diamanti. Almost no tourists. The wife will want to walk every street.
Train: Vicenza → Padova → Ferrara ~1.5h

Mantova
Gonzaga Renaissance

A UNESCO Renaissance city ringed by three lakes — the Palazzo Ducale and Palazzo Te are among Italy's finest rooms
Gonzaga family's Palazzo Ducale is a 500-room palace. Palazzo Te has Giulio Romano's insane frescoed Sala dei Giganti — visually overwhelming in the best way. The whole city sits on lake peninsulas. Quiet.
Train: Vicenza → Verona → Mantova ~1.5h

Milano
Navigli & the Duomo

The one big-city pick — Duomo rooftop, Galleria, Sforza Castle, Navigli canal aperitivo, great restaurant scene
