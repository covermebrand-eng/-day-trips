i.

The weather.

Abano sits in the Po valley at 15m elevation — warmer and flatter than any alpine destination. Pick any weekend, the pools are heated regardless. Summer is ideal, spring and autumn are fine, even winter works.

This trip is weather-proof. All thermal pools are heated 30–34°C year-round from volcanic springs. A rainy Saturday in Abano is still a great spa day — rain just keeps it quieter.

Things worth knowing.

  • Weather-proof trip. This is the only overnight on the list that works equally well in November or July. Pools are volcanic spring-fed and heated year-round.
  • Hydrate constantly. Thermal water at 33°C depletes you fast, especially in summer. Drink water before, during, and after every pool session.
  • Check-in is 2:30–3:00 PM at most Abano hotels. You can arrive earlier and use the spa from check-in — just ask at reception.
  • Abano vs. Montegrotto. Both are on the same train line (same station "Terme Euganee–Abano-Montegrotto"). Abano is the bigger, slightly posher town. Montegrotto is quieter. Both are walkable from the station.
  • Tourist tax at Italian hotels: a few euros per person per night, paid in cash at checkout. Keep coins on you.
  • Thermal pool etiquette: shower before entering any pool, no running, no shaving in pools (they'll ask you to leave), no food poolside.
  • Booking breakfast rate: always filter for "breakfast included" before booking — it's not always the default displayed option.
